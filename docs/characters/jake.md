# Jake — Character Profile

## Basic Info

- **Name:** Jake
- **Age:** Early 30s (about the PC's age, maybe a year or two older)
- **Occupation:** Unknown. Works from coffee shops. Has a laptop. Keeps hours that suggest remote/freelance.
- **Neighborhood:** Same area as the PC. Lives near Lexington Ave / Union and Third.
- **Appearance:** Good shoulders. The kind of jaw that gets noticed. Tall — taller than the PC expected when she sees him standing on the street vs. sitting at a table. Forearms that her eyes follow before her brain catches up. Holds his phone but doesn't look at it.

## Personality

Jake is unhurried. He doesn't perform — not his interest, not his casualness, not his attention. He smiles without calculating. He remembers things without making a show of it. His "hey" is just a "hey."

**Speech patterns:**
- Short. Warm but not effusive. "Could be worse." "Good taste." "Yeah?"
- Doesn't over-talk. Leaves room. The silence between his words isn't awkward — it's just space.
- When he's interested, he asks a real question. When he's not pushing, he doesn't.
- "Okay" is his acceptance word. He says it like things are already decided and he's just confirming.

**Social style:**
- Makes the first move but doesn't pressure. Pulls out a chair, but it's "you should sit" not "come sit with me."
- Reads the PC well — backs off when she's quiet, engages when she's open.
- Doesn't require reciprocity. If she waves and walks past, he returns it without complication.

## What He Represents

Jake is the **"she chose this"** register. The tenderness thread. Not urgency, not unpredictability — the slow build of a connection she elected to pursue. Coffee shop to sidewalk to date to intimacy, each step something she agreed to.

He's the counterpoint to the stranger encounters and the Marcus tension. With Jake, the body's responses are invited. The transformation texture is about *discovery with someone safe* — learning what this body does when she's with someone she trusts, rather than someone the world threw at her.

## Relationship Progression

1. **Coffee shop** — They meet in line. Small talk or silence. She can sit with him or leave.
2. **Coffee shop return** — He's there again. She acknowledges him or doesn't. Parallel working.
3. **Street encounter** — Different context. He's taller than she expected. Standing, not sitting. The body recalibrates.
4. **First date** — He asked. She said yes. A restaurant or bar, evening. The transition from acquaintance to intention.
5. **Second date** — His suggestion. Casual. Physical contact arrives — hand on back, arm touch. The first kiss (if she allows it).
6. **Intimacy** — Her apartment or his. First sexual experience in this body with someone she chose. Discovery, not performance.
7. **Morning after** — Waking up next to him. What changed. What didn't.

## Key Dynamics

- **He doesn't know about the transformation.** He met her as she is. His attraction is to the woman in front of him. He has no context for her internal experience.
- **He's patient.** If she pulls back, he doesn't push. If she engages, he meets her. This is what makes him safe — and what makes the intimacy meaningful.
- **He's observant but not analytical.** He notices things (she takes sugar, she's usually there Tuesdays, she gets quiet when something's on her mind) without performing the observation.

## Trait Interactions

- **SHY PC:** The silence with Jake is comfortable, not charged. He doesn't require her to perform. The physical escalation is slower and each step is louder.
- **FLIRTY PC:** She sets the pace. He matches. The dynamic is playful. She knows what she's doing even when the body's responses surprise her.
- **CONFIDENT PC:** Direct. Asks real questions. Steers conversations. The attraction is mutual competence and mutual honesty.
- **ANALYTICAL PC:** Catalogs everything about him and can't stop. His jaw, his hands, his timing. The analysis is both coping mechanism and foreplay.
- **ROMANTIC PC:** Imagines forward. What this could become. The coffee shop as origin story.

## Voice Notes

When writing Jake's dialogue:
- Short sentences. No speeches.
- Warm without trying. Not performing warmth.
- He says things once. Doesn't repeat or elaborate unless asked.
- His humor is dry, not witty. Observational, not constructed.
- He doesn't explain himself. "Okay" means he's on board. "Yeah?" means he's interested. That's enough.
